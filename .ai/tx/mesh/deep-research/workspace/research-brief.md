# Research Brief

**Date**: 2025-12-05
**Status**: Ready for research

## Research Topic

Successful community building patterns for open source software projects, with specific focus on CLI/developer tools and Fediverse-first strategies.

## Scope

### In Scope
- OSS community building frameworks and methodologies
- Case studies of successful OSS CLI/developer tools (2020-2025)
- Fediverse-specific strategies (Mastodon, Bluesky)
- Platform-specific tactics (Discord, Reddit, HN, GitHub, Mastodon, Bluesky)
- Time-efficient strategies optimized for 15-minute daily engagement
- Pre-launch vs post-launch community strategies
- Specific target communities for know-cli (with direct links, member counts, culture assessment)
- Growth stages: 0→100, 100→1000, 1000→10000 users
- Risk mitigation and success metrics

### Out of Scope
- General marketing strategies not specific to OSS
- Paid advertising approaches
- Projects older than 2020 (unless landmark case studies)
- Non-developer-tool OSS projects
- Centralized social media platforms (Twitter/X, Facebook, LinkedIn) as primary channels

## Research Objectives

1. Identify proven community building frameworks/methodologies specifically for OSS projects
2. Extract actionable tactics from 5-7 successful OSS CLI/developer tool case studies launched 2020-2025
3. Develop comprehensive Fediverse strategy guide for Mastodon and Bluesky community building
4. Document platform-specific best practices for Discord, Reddit, Hacker News, GitHub, Mastodon, and Bluesky
5. Design time-efficient engagement playbook optimized for 15-minute daily blocks
6. Define optimal pre-launch timeline and differentiate pre vs post-launch strategies
7. Identify and vet specific target communities (Discord servers, Reddit subreddits, Mastodon instances, Bluesky communities, GitHub orgs)
8. Establish success metrics, KPIs, and risk mitigation strategies

## Key Questions to Answer

1. What are the critical success factors and common failure patterns at each community growth stage (0→100, 100→1000, 1000→10000)?
2. How did successful CLI/developer tools acquire their first 100 users, and what tactics worked vs didn't?
3. How does community building differ on Fediverse vs centralized platforms, and what are best practices for Mastodon and Bluesky engagement?
4. What high-leverage activities can be done in 15-minute blocks to maximize community impact with minimal time?
5. What is the optimal pre-launch duration and how do you maintain momentum post-launch while avoiding "launch spike then crash"?
6. Which specific Discord servers, Reddit subreddits, Mastodon instances, Bluesky communities, and GitHub organizations should know-cli target?
7. What are the platform-specific rules, norms, and anti-spam tactics for each target platform?
8. What automation tools and tactics can be used without being perceived as spammy?
9. How do you build authenticity in short time windows?
10. What are the key inflection points in OSS community growth and what triggers them?

## Target Audience

- **Primary**: AI developers and agent builders interested in launching know-cli
- **Secondary**: Software architects and developer tool creators
- **User Profile**: Technical founder with ~15 minutes/day for community engagement, Fediverse preference, pre-launch phase
